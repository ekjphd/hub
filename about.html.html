Leadership Team

Dr. Emily Kuzneski Johnson

Principal Investigator | Associate Professor, English Department & Texts and Technology Ph.D. Program

Dr. Johnson is an Associate Professor in the Department of English (Technical Communication and Digital Humanities) and core faculty in the Texts and Technology Ph.D. program. She is co-author of Critical Making in the Age of AI (with Anastasia Salter, Amherst, 2025) and Playful Pedagogy in the Pandemic: Pivoting to Games-Based Learning (Routledge, 2022). Her research focuses on digital humanities, technical communication, user-centered design, critical making, and educational technology. Dr. Johnson is currently Principal Investigator of a U.S. Department of Education-funded project investigating the efficacy of video games for language learning.

Dr. Anastasia Salter

Co-Principal Investigator | Professor & Director of Graduate Programs, College of Arts and Humanities

Dr. Salter is a Professor of English and the Director of Graduate Programs and the Ph.D. in Texts & Technology for the College of Arts and Humanities. She is the author of numerous books including A Portrait of the Auteur as Fanboy (University of Mississippi Press, 2020), Adventure Games: Playing the Outsider (Bloomsbury, 2020), and Toxic Geek Masculinity in Media (Palgrave Macmillan, 2017). Her research spans electronic literature, adventure games, platform studies, digital humanities, and media studies. Dr. Salter currently serves as President of the Electronic Literature Organization.

Dr. Mel Stanfill

Co-Principal Investigator | Associate Professor & Program Coordinator, Texts and Technology Ph.D. Program

Dr. Stanfill is an Associate Professor in the Texts & Technology Program and Department of English. Their research examines the complex interactions between individuals and various media forms, with particular focus on how technology, identity, law, and economics shape cultural access and interpretation. Dr. Stanfill is the author of Fandom Is Ugly: Networked Harassment in Participatory Culture (NYU Press, 2024), which won the 2025 National Communication Association Diamond Anniversary Book Award, and Rock This Way: Cultural Constructions of Musical Legitimacy (University of Michigan Press, 2023). They serve as Co-Editor of the Transformative Works and Cultures journal.

Project Staff

The Constellation Hub is supported by doctoral students from UCF's Texts and Technology Ph.D. Program, an interdisciplinary program at the intersection of humanities and technology. Our student staff bring expertise across six specialized pathways: Digital Humanities, Digital Media, Editing, Publishing, and Interdisciplinary Curating (EPIC), Public History, Rhetoric and Composition, and Scientific and Technical Communication.

Our Institutional Home

The Constellation Hub is housed within the College of Arts and Humanities at the University of Central Florida, one of the nation's largest universities with a commitment to innovation, diversity, and community engagement. The project represents a collaboration between the Department of English and the Texts and Technology Ph.D. Program, bringing together expertise in digital humanities, technical communication, media studies, and public scholarship.
